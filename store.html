Butcher Block Table

"Butcher Block on wheels," created by the artist David Flynn. The table features a thick, square butcher block top with a beautiful checkerboard pattern of light and dark wood tones, showcasing the end grain of the wood—this type of construction is known for its durability and resistance to cuts and scratches. The block is stamped with the word "CATSKILL" on the side, indicating either the brand or the wood's origin. The base of the table consists of slender, metal legs that have been decoratively turned to add an element of elegance to what is traditionally a very functional piece of furniture. These legs are dark in color, which contrasts nicely with the warm wood tones of the butcher block. There is a lower shelf made of metal mesh, which provides additional storage space and adds to the industrial aesthetic of the piece. The table is also equipped with caster wheels, making it mobile and versatile for use in various kitchen settings. The overall design suggests a blend of utility and artistry, combining the robust functionality of a butcher block with the intricate craftsmanship of metalwork.

End Table

"End Table" crafted by the artist David Flynn. The table boasts a square top made of what appears to be marble or a similarly patterned stone, characterized by a mottled surface with various hues that could include creams, browns, and grays, which give it a rich, organic appearance. Below the top, the table features a metal apron with a textured, corrugated front, adding depth and interest to the design. This apron surrounds the top and contributes to the table's robust structure while providing a visual transition from the smooth stone surface to the metalwork below. The table stands on four elegantly curved metal legs. These legs exhibit traditional blacksmithing techniques, with a tapered and twisted design that speaks to the artist's skill in metalwork. The legs are adorned with subtle ornamental details such as bulbs and spirals, which enhance the table's artistic value. The feet of the table are particularly noteworthy; they curl outward in a distinctive scroll-like fashion, offering both stability and a flourish that complements the overall aesthetic. The dark patina of the metal contrasts beautifully with the stone top and would make this end table a statement piece in any interior setting. The table, as depicted, is a testament to the fusion of materials and the harmony between the earthy quality of the stone and the crafted metal. It exemplifies the ability of the artist to combine functional furniture design with artistic blacksmithing to create a unique and attractive piece of furniture.

Lanterns

A set of two decorative lanterns titled "Lanterns" by the artist David Flynn. The lanterns are suspended from a metal stand that features an overarching horizontal beam supported by a vertical pole, all demonstrating a dark patina finish that signifies metalwork. On the left, there is a smaller lantern with a traditional shape, holding a yellow-tinted glass pane. It has a curved metal top and bottom with intricate scrollwork that adds to its classic charm. The lantern is hung by a small chain attached to the beam, which allows it to dangle freely, giving it a quaint, old-world feel. To the right is a larger, more stylized lantern with a bold blue-tinted glass pane that contrasts sharply with the yellow of its counterpart. This lantern's design is more elaborate and modern, with metal strips that curve outward in a flame-like fashion, suggesting movement and a certain natural fluidity. The top is accented with what appears to be leaf-like metalwork, adding to its ornate appearance. Like the smaller lantern, it is also hung from the beam, but it is supported in a fixed position rather than dangling. Both lanterns exhibit Flynn's craftsmanship in shaping metal to frame the colored glass, resulting in functional lighting fixtures that also serve as pieces of art. The design elements suggest a blend of both traditional and contemporary styles, appealing to various tastes and suitable for different settings. The lanterns, with their colored glass, would cast a warm, inviting light, while their metal frames contribute to their durability and artistic presentation.
